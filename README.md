T+0 Settlement
In traditional finance the most common settlement mechanics is T+1. Which means case and security officially exchanges hand one business days (+1) after the trade happened (T). This implies that after the sales of a security, investors are not able to access their funds within the same day. If I person wants to sell some of their stocks on Friday night, after traditional exchange closes, they'll need to wait until at least Tuesday the next week to withdraw their funds. By using the atomic settlement mechanics, Hyperliquid is able to accomplish near instant settlements of securities and even offer their ever own instrument: perpetual futures.  

I want to create a platform that combines Klarna and a traditional exchange where people can use their securities as collaterals in order to access their funds right away. [not written by AI]
